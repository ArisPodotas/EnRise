Turning waste into valuable products
Deforestation
Each year 6 million trees are needed to fuel Greece's paper consumption and more so worldwide 15 billion trees each year are used to make paper. Estimates say the global tree count has fallen by 46% since the beginning of human civilization. This massive loss affects everything but most importantly our climate and the biodiversity of the planet
 
Waste and pollution
The agricultural sector throws away secondary products, rich in Bio-energy en-mass. These materials could be applied to better causes but the agricultureal sector sees them as a burden rather than an asset. This problem isnt unique to the producers, one third of all food produced is lost or wasted. Around 1.3 billion tonnes of food costing close to $940 billion each year. Up to 10% of global greenhouse gases comes from food that is produced, but not eaten.

Have you noticed the price of paper going up in recent years?
This is because, thought the number of trees worldwide has grown the ammount of effective trees for creating paper has decreased. All the new trees are a result of efforts to fight climate change and the resulting trees are too young for harvesting, thus, the supply is becoming all the more scarce
This works to illustrate how much deforestation actually affects us, and that the efforts to fight it, though great, are not really a solution as much as they are a temporary fix. The real way to solve this crysis is to stop deforestation completly
